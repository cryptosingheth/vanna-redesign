The keys behind the lock: validating every execute().

Leverage, collateral, borrow limits and health factor — the four checks the Risk Engine runs before a single asset moves. Fail any one, and the call reverts.

Composable credit is powerful precisely because it moves freely. That freedom only works if there's a gate between intent and settlement that can say no. On Vanna, that gate is the Risk Engine, and it runs four checks on every single execute() before any asset moves.

Check 1 — Leverage

The account can borrow up to 10× (900% LTV), and not a cent more. The engine computes the leverage the requested action would create and rejects anything past the cap. This is the outer wall: no trade can ever put the account beyond the protocol's maximum risk.

Check 2 — Collateral

Is the collateral supported, and can it be priced right now? The engine confirms the asset is on the accepted list (USDC, XLM, ETH, WBTC, EURC…) and that the multi-oracle system has a fresh, in-consensus mark for it. Stale or out-of-consensus prices fail the check — you can't borrow against a number the engine doesn't trust.

The oracle is part of the check
Chainlink, Pyth and RedStone are queried together. Any feed more than ~2% off the median is discarded, and marks are TWAP-gated. A collateral value only counts if the oracles agree on it.

Check 3 — Borrow limit

Credit is real, borrowed liquidity — so it's bounded by what the lending pool can actually supply. The engine checks the draw against pool utilization and the account's remaining borrow capacity. This protects lenders as much as the protocol: you can't borrow liquidity that isn't there.

Check 4 — Health factor

The last and most important question: after this action settles, does the account's netted Health Factor stay above 1.0? The engine simulates the post-trade state and refuses anything that would open the account into the liquidation zone.

the gate
if (leverage > MAX_LEVERAGE)        revert('leverage')
if (!collateralSupported || stale)  revert('collateral')
if (borrow > poolCapacity)          revert('borrow-limit')
if (postTradeHF < 1.0)              revert('health-factor')
// all clear → settle on-chain
The lock has four keys. Turn all four and the door opens on-chain. Miss one and the call reverts — no half-executed trade, no asset left in limbo.

Why 'revert' is the whole point

There's no partial pass. Validation is atomic: either every check clears and the action settles, or the transaction reverts and the account is exactly as it was. That atomicity is what lets Vanna offer 10× credit across a dozen venues without a dozen places for things to go quietly wrong.

A note on risk
These checks bound risk at trade time; they don't remove it. Prices still move, interest still accrues, and volatile markets can still stress an account after it opens — which is exactly what the guardian and liquidation systems are for.
