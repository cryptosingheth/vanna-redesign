Inside the Smart Margin Account: one account, every venue.

A single Soroban smart account routes execute() calls to Blend, Soroswap and Aquarius — while netting risk in one place. Here's how the account, the router, and cross-margin fit together.

In most of DeFi your positions live in five wallets across ten tabs. Vanna collapses that into one Smart Margin Account: a smart-contract wallet that holds your collateral and your borrowed credit, and speaks to every venue through a single call — execute().

This piece is the engineering tour: the account itself, how one call reaches Blend, Soroswap or Aquarius, and why risk is measured once — for the whole account — instead of position by position.

The account is a programmable wallet

The Smart Margin Account is built on account abstraction (a Soroban smart account on Stellar; ERC-4337-style on EVM chains). That buys three things a plain EOA can't offer:

  • Gasless UX — Vanna can sponsor gas, so users act without holding the native token for fees.
  • Session keys — approve once, then trade all day without re-signing every step.
  • Modularity — new venues plug in as adapters without migrating the account.
Collateral and credit, together
Both your deposited collateral and your borrowed credit sit inside the same account. That's what makes the credit composable: the account can deploy it anywhere Vanna routes to, without shuffling funds between wallets first.

One call, every venue

You never call a venue directly. You call execute() on your account with an intent, and the router resolves it to the right adapter — Blend for lending, Soroswap for swaps, Aquarius for AMM liquidity, Derive for options, and so on.

account.execute()
vanna.execute({
  venue: "blend",         // resolved to the Blend adapter
  action: "supply",
  asset: "XLM",
  amount: 1_000,
  risk: "conservative",   // HF floor the engine must respect
})

The same shape of call opens a 3× long on Soroswap, supplies to a Blend pool, or provides liquidity on Aquarius. The venue changes; the account, the accounting, and the risk model do not.

Risk is measured once — for the account

Here's the part that changes everything: because every position settles back into the same account, Vanna computes one netted Health Factor for the whole thing. Cross-margin, not isolated margin.

netted, not isolated
Long ETH perp      +$500 unrealized
Short BTC perp     −$300 unrealized
──────────────────────────────
Net exposure       +$200
→ HF computed on the net, not the worst leg

A loss on one leg is offset by a gain on another before the engine decides you're at risk. That's why the same account can be long spot, short a perp to hedge, and farming yield — all at once — and still be judged by a single, honest number.

One account, one health factor, every venue. The account is the unit of risk — so composability and safety stop fighting each other.

Why it matters

The Smart Margin Account is the reason Vanna's credit is composable rather than just borrowed. It's a DeFi credit card you swipe across protocols — with the risk desk built into the card itself.
