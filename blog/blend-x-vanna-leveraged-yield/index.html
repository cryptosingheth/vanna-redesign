Blend is a capital-efficient lending pool on Stellar. On its own, supplying XLM earns you the base pool APR. Wire it to Vanna's composable credit and you can loop that position to roughly 3× — without ever leaving the pool, and without juggling wallets.

This is the walkthrough: the loop, the honest math after borrow cost, and the single health factor that keeps the whole thing inside the safe zone.

The math after borrow cost

Leverage cuts both ways, so the number that matters is the net yield — pool APR on the levered position, minus the borrow APR on the credit you used. Because Vanna's credit is real borrowed liquidity, you pay a simple borrow APR — no perp funding fees, no entry/exit trading fees.

effective APR (illustrative)
Base Blend supply APR        ~11%
× 3× looped exposure         ~33% gross
− borrow cost on 2× credit   ~ (2 × 8%) = 16%
──────────────────────────────────────
≈ net APR on your capital    ~17%
Rates are dynamic
Borrow APR moves with pool utilization (roughly 5–15%), and Blend's supply APR moves with demand. The loop is attractive while the spread between them is positive — which is exactly the number Vanna surfaces before you commit.

The health factor keeps it honest

A 3× loop still opens at a healthy HF 2.8 — not because 3× is timid, but because it's collateralised leverage priced with a buffer, not a hair-trigger perp. The Risk Engine watches it 24/7, and if the spread inverts or XLM sells off, the guardian de-risks before the line rather than at it.
