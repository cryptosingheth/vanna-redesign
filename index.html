Isolated vs unified

Same book. Same crash.
Different endings.

One trader, three legs: a 2× long, a short hedge, a yield loop. In isolated margin each leg stands alone — the hedge’s profit is stranded in another account while the long gets liquidated. In a unified account the same legs net first. Drag the shock.

Deployable, not withdrawable

Credit that can move,
but never leave.

The reason Vanna can extend credit beyond your collateral: borrowed funds never exit the protocol’s perimeter. They deploy to approved venues through one authorised execute() path — and every position they open is held as a marked claim the engine can always see, price, and seize.

✓Deploy — spot, perps, yield, lending: any approved venue, one account.
✓Recall — positions are marked claims; the engine can unwind them to repay debt.
✕Withdraw — borrowed funds cannot cross the boundary. Ever. That is the contract.
✕ withdraw reverted
credit is use-constrained: it can compound inside the perimeter, it cannot exfiltrate — which is what makes >1× leverage extendable against 1× collateral
